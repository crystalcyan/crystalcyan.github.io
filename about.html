Crystal Yan

Crystal Yan is a product leader focused on creating civic and social impact by designing human-centered products, teams, and organizations.


Currently, she is a Product Lead at Remitly, a mobile financial services company in Seattle that helps immigrants send money to loved ones across borders. In her role on the New Initiatives team, she leads teams to launch new ventures and financial services products for immigrants and their families. Previously, she was a Senior Manager leading product and UX teams at United States Digital Service at the White House in Washington DC, a startup within the federal government using design and technology to deliver better government services to the public. Through the US Digital Service, Crystal advised senior management at USCIS on strategies to reduce the asylum backlog for the US asylum program for 729,000 asylum seekers, and policy leaders at CMS on how to make it easier for Medicare to pay for value-based care for 57 million patients. Prior to that, she was part of the founding team at FiscalNote, a software, data, and media company that helps advocacy teams bring predictive analytics to politics and policy and transforms how organizations interact with governments worldwide. Through several leadership roles in product, design, sales, and marketing, she helped the company achieve initial product-market fit, grow from 15 to 150+ employees, and launch a new multimillion dollar product portfolio that tripled average contract value (ACV).


She has worked in the US, India, China, and Cambodia and spoken at technology and design conferences across the US, Canada, Mexico, Chile, China, India, Ireland, Germany, Norway, Portugal, the UK, Serbia, and Croatia. She has consulted for MyHealthEd and Rise, and is a faculty member at the School of Visual Concepts.
